🔴 Enroll → Start: Worst Gate
3,100 learners (45.6%) enrolled but never pressed play. This is the product's most critical failure point — likely onboarding friction or poor first-session design.
🟢 Start → Complete: Solid
Of the 3,700 who started, 75% eventually completed. Once learners engage, momentum carries them through — the content is not the problem.
📌 Fix Activation First
A 10% improvement in activation (enroll→start) would add ~680 new starters and approximately 510 more completions, without acquiring a single new visitor.

All 3 beginner courses hit ~60% completion with ~3.82 avg satisfaction. These are the product's anchors — keep them polished and use them as entry points.
🔴 Advanced courses struggling
Python, A/B Testing, and Cohort Analysis all sit below 26% completion. High enrolment (2,250 combined) but poor throughput — a readiness mismatch problem.
📌 Prerequisite paths needed
Advanced course learners may be under-prepared. Consider gating advanced enrolments with beginner completions, or adding structured learning paths.

⚠ Session cliff at 1–2
3,638 learners (53%) are stuck at 1–2 sessions with 0% completion. Getting them to session 6 is the single highest-leverage engagement lever.
✅ 6+ sessions = near-certain completion
Learners who hit 6+ sessions complete at 96–99%. The product works — the challenge is getting learners to that threshold.
📌 Day 30 retention at 34.7%
A 13.6 point drop from Day 1 to Day 30. Typical for async course products, but signals need for nudge sequences, streak mechanics, or cohort accountability features.

⚠ Mobile is 56% — but trailing
Most learners use mobile (3,808 enrollments) yet complete at only 36.2% vs 47.8% for desktop. A 11.5pt gap on the majority audience is a critical product risk.
✅ 35–44 age group leads
This group has the highest completion (42.1%) despite not being the largest segment — likely higher career motivation and focused learning habits.
📌 Gauteng dominates volume
Gauteng has 2,360 enrollments (34.7% of total) but ranks 6th in completion. Improving this province's completion rate would have the highest absolute impact.

🔴 45.6% never start
"Enrolled not started" is by far the largest drop-off bucket. Email sequences, push notifications, or a better course start experience could directly recover this cohort.
⚠ Advanced courses: 74–77% dropout
Python, A/B Testing, and Cohort Analysis have the worst dropout rates. These learners may be enrolling out of curiosity but lack the prerequisite skills to persist.
📌 Mid-course drop is manageable
Only 7.8% drop mid-course. This is a solvable problem with progress reminders, milestone celebrations, or cohort-based check-ins at the halfway point.
